# VMS Performance Management System
## Executive Video Demo Script

**Duration:** ~2:50 minutes  
**Audience:** C-Suite Executives  
**Tone:** Strategic with Technical Substance

---

## OPENING (0:00 - 0:15)

*[Visual: CEO Dashboard animating into view with company-wide metrics]*

**SCRIPT:**

> "This is the VMS Performance Management System—an integrated platform that connects job architecture, balanced scorecards, quarterly reviews, and 360-degree feedback into a single performance ecosystem.
>
> Let me show you how it works."

---

## JOB DESCRIPTIONS & ORG STRUCTURE (0:15 - 0:45)

*[Visual: Job Description form → reporting hierarchy → org chart]*

**SCRIPT:**

> "Every performance cycle starts with **Job Descriptions**—but these aren't static documents.
>
> Each role defines reporting relationships, authority limits, qualification requirements, and critically—**Key Result Areas** organized under Balanced Scorecard perspectives: Financial, Customer, Internal Processes, and Learning & Growth.
>
> The org chart is generated automatically from these relationships. When a JD changes, the hierarchy updates. When you assign an employee to a role, their BSC inherits the KRAs directly—no duplicate data entry."

---

## BALANCED SCORECARD: THE SCORING ENGINE (0:45 - 1:20)

*[Visual: BSC form with KRAs → score calculation → grade output]*

**SCRIPT:**

> "The **Balanced Scorecard** runs on a weighted formula. Each perspective carries a configurable weight—typically 25% each. Within perspectives, individual KRAs have their own weights.
>
> Employees enter targets and actuals. The system calculates achievement percentage—with support for both 'maximize' metrics like revenue and 'minimize' metrics like cost overruns, where the formula inverts.
>
> Scores roll up automatically. A weighted total of 120% or higher grades as A+. Below 50% flags as F. The ten-point grading scale ties directly to bonus entitlement percentages—giving you a defensible, consistent compensation link.
>
> Quarterly and annual periods are both supported, with annual reviews aggregating quarterly data."

---

## PEAR: STRUCTURED QUARTERLY 1:1s (1:20 - 1:50)

*[Visual: PEAR form sections → performance factors → OKRs → core values grid]*

**SCRIPT:**

> "**PEAR**—Performance Evaluation Assessment Review—structures your quarterly 1:1 conversations.
>
> Nine performance factor categories with sub-items. Both employee self-ratings and manager ratings on a standardized scale. The BSC score for that quarter pulls in automatically as a reference point.
>
> OKRs are tracked with challenge ratings and alignment scores. Core values map to your company's specific framework—in this case, the ACT-REF model. And there's a dedicated section for development needs that carries forward."

---

## 360-DEGREE FEEDBACK: MULTI-RATER INPUT (1:50 - 2:10)

*[Visual: 360 survey interface → response aggregation → PEAR integration]*

**SCRIPT:**

> "**360-Degree Surveys** collect feedback from multiple raters—peers, direct reports, supervisors.
>
> Responses are anonymous and aggregate into section-level scores. The system calculates averages across all reviewers and displays variance.
>
> Here's the integration point: 360 feedback flows directly into the PEAR review, so managers have that context when conducting quarterly discussions. It's connected, not siloed."

---

## CEO DASHBOARD: ORGANIZATIONAL ANALYTICS (2:10 - 2:40)

*[Visual: CEO Dashboard → period selector → department drill-down → employee table → grade distribution chart]*

**SCRIPT:**

> "The **CEO Dashboard** aggregates everything.
>
> Filter by period—quarterly or annual. See completion rates across all review types: BSC, PEAR, 360, Life Wheel. Average scores display at organization level with department breakdowns.
>
> Click into any department—you get employee-level detail: individual BSC and PEAR scores, their grades, and a flag for anyone below threshold.
>
> Grade distribution charts show you the shape of performance across the company. Are you top-heavy with A-performers? Is there a concerning cluster at C or below? The data answers it."

---

## CLOSING (2:40 - 2:50)

*[Visual: Dashboard with all metrics visible]*

**SCRIPT:**

> "One system. Job architecture to scorecards to reviews to executive reporting.
>
> That's VMS Performance Management."

## PRODUCTION NOTES

### Screen Flow Sequence:
1. CEO Dashboard landing (3 sec)
2. Job Description form — show KRA section with BSC perspectives (10 sec)
3. Org chart with reporting lines (5 sec)
4. BSC entry — show target/actual fields, weighted calculation, grade output (12 sec)
5. PEAR form — performance factors, OKR section, core values grid (10 sec)
6. 360 survey — response list, aggregated scores (8 sec)
7. CEO Dashboard — period filter, department click, employee table, grade chart (15 sec)
8. Final dashboard wide shot (3 sec)

### Key Technical Features to Highlight:
- **KRA inheritance** from JD to BSC (auto-population)
- **Weighted score formula** (perspective weight × KRA weight × achievement %)
- **Maximize/Minimize toggle** for measurement direction
- **Ten-point grading scale** with configurable thresholds
- **Period filtering** — quarterly and annual aggregation
- **Cross-system data flow** — BSC → PEAR, 360 → PEAR
- **Completion rate tracking** per review type

### Sample Data Points to Display:
- Total Employees: 85
- BSC Completion: 78/85 (92%)
- PEAR Completion: 71/85 (84%)
- Avg BSC Score: 87.3%
- Grade Distribution: A+ (8), A (24), A- (19), B+ (15), B (9), C+ (3), C (2), below C (5)

### Voice-Over Guidance:
- Matter-of-fact delivery — let the system speak for itself
- Slow down slightly on technical terms (weighted formula, maximize/minimize)
- Natural pauses when transitioning between modules

### Optional Background:
- Subtle ambient music or none
- Keep focus on the walkthrough
